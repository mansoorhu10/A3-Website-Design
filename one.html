Main Types of Sushi

Type Description Image
Nigiri A topping, typically fish, which is served on top of sushi rice.
Maki Rice and filling wrapped in seaweed, and what most people imagine when they think of sushi.
Uramaki Similar to maki, however, the rice is on the outside and seaweed wraps the filling.
Temaki Sushi that has been rolled into a cone shape with rice and filling inside.

Sushi vs. Sashimi

Sashimi is just the meat itself, typically fish, that is served without any rice or other ingredients. Sushi, on the other hand, has the meat, adds rice, and can have vegetables, sauces, and more. Technically speaking, Sashimi is not considered a type of sushi because of the fact that it. does not have vinegar seasoned rice served with it, which is the most important part of sushi. Nigiri can be considered halfway in between the two of them, as it is sashimi served on top of rectangular-shaped sushi rice.
